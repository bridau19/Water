What is a water footprint?

the total amount of fresh water used to produce the goods and services consumed by an individual in a certain amount of time

green water footprint: amount of rainwater required, either evaporated or used directly, to make an item

grey water footprint:
amount of freshwater required to dilute the wastewater generated in manufacturing for maintaining water quality

blue water footprint:
amount of surface water and groundwater required, either evaporated or used directly, to produce an item

Direct Water Use and Virtual Water Use

Direct water is the water necessary to carry out an activity. This water is directly involoved in a process at a given time or location
Examples include water used in a washing machine or to boil pasta

Virtual water is the water used to produce food, generate energy, and manufacture consumer products
Examples include water used for drilling or to produce energy

Knowing your water footprint is important to keep you aware of the water use and provide a frame of reference for improving how you use water. Business and organizations measure their water footprint to reveal patterns in water use and find ways to be more efficient, cut costs, ensure less water contamination, and increase sustainable practixes.
